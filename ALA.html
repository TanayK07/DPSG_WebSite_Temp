The Assam Legislative Assembly came into being on the day of its first sitting on April 7, 1937. The Legislative Assembly is empowered to raise Bills which after passing become Acts. The National Register of Citizens (NRC) has been implemented only in the state of Assam which is meant to identify a bona fide citizen and by the order of the Supreme Court of India, it is being currently updated to detect Foreign nationals who might have entered the State illegally after the midnight of March 24, 197 1. While NRC does not distinguish migrants on the basis of religion, the Citizenship Amendment Bill, 2016 is designed to grant citizenship to non-Muslim refugees persecuted in neighboring countries. The Bill seeks to grant citizenship to people without valid documents belonging to six religions (Hindus, Sikhs, Buddhists, Jains, Parsis and Christians) from three countries (Afghanistan, Bangladesh and Pakistan) after six years of residence in India arriving on or before December 31, 2014. Therefore, discussing the viability of the Citizenship Amendment Act in the Assam Vidhan Sabha will be a highly politically electrifying issue as there will be a lot of opposition from some sectors.
